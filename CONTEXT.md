## Core Argument

**Leadership density theory of movement resource conversion:**

Organizations that convert mobilization moments into durable policy influence have sufficient density of "purple line" leaders—volunteers who:
- Participate in 5+ organizational activities per year
- Recruit and develop new leaders themselves

**Key insight:** The participation-power gap is a function of organizational leader density, not mobilization intensity.

## Threshold Findings (Critical!)

| Purple Line Leaders | Base Multiplier | 2025 Outcomes |
|---------------------|-----------------|---------------|
| **200-700** | 3x or larger | Statewide or cross-local wins |
| **80-100** | 2x or larger | Local campaign wins |
| **<50** | Any size | No policy translation |

*Consistent across red, blue, and purple states*

## Purple Line Origin Story

Members of the participatory action project tracked committed leaders over time, co-developing the metric. Called "purple line" because of the color first used in longitudinal data visualizations:
- **Grey line:** Cumulative people who participated once
- **Blue line:** People who participated 2-4 times
- **Purple line:** People who participated 5+ times (highly committed)
